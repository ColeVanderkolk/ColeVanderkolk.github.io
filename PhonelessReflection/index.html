Phoneless Reflection

Turns out its not that bad

With a generation of people stuck to their phones, these days it seems like the idea of leaving your phone behind would be as important as leaving your keys, wallet, or any other vital things you may need. its only once you put your phone down for a moment that you realize for thousands of years people havent been able to call eachother, track eachother, look up directions, etc. that was one of the most eye opening things for me. I have never been that glueed to my phone, thankfully I have set boundaries that make sure that I am not just on my screen all day scrolling away my life though im sure I would if I tried. I think that being away from the short form content that im used to was probably the most dramatic difference in my life, even though I dont do much I underestimated how boring the bus is without music, how long bathroom breaks actually are without my games, And how it doesnt matter how long until the next bus is going to be here... im gonna have to wait anyways. I didnt tell anyone that I was putting my phone away for a day besides my girlfriend who I live with, mainly becuase we use our phones to enter our building, but part of me felt kind of relieved. I imagine that back before the day of email and text message life was a lot slower paced and calming, you wouldnt get emails letting you know how youre behind in 100 different ways, and how everyone else is doing, you could literally only worry about your own life and maybe a select few of the people around you because unless you actually heard it from their mouth you would never know. for me also the biggest disruptor was not always knowing the time, always checking how many hours minutes seconds until whatever I was doing was done and I had to go do something else, how many precious minutes could I spend doing the things I want to do before I went to do the things I had to do. it felt like some pressure was lifted to be perfectly on point for everything and instead I could go back to the realm of approximately. I didnt have to worry about competing with others I was just in my own space with nothing but the people directly around me. showing that no matter how many followers, text groups, or podcasts I had, the only people that would be there if anything were to happen were literally those sitting right next to me. It also made it painstakingly aware that unlike back in the day when you were most likely to make friends with those you shared a commute with, or worked with, or were around consistently, instead our lives are formed by the people that we are not around in those moments, that most of my social group isnt regularily around me and that even though ive seen the same people on my bus route, at my bus stop at the same time for weeks, I'll likely never know their name becuase their just the bus people... and I'm listening to my music.
